## CLASS OVERVIEW

### Class/Spec Name and Role
- **Guardian Druid** - Tank specialization

### Strengths and Weaknesses
#### Strengths:
- High single-target damage in raid
- High AoE/Mythic+ damage
- Large health pool
- Short cooldowns result in stable damage intake
- Stackable, no-cooldown active mitigation via **Ironfur**
- Flexible hybrid off-healing capabilities through **After the Wildfire** and **Dream of Cenarius**
- Good raid utility with **Rebirth**, **Stampeding Roar**, and **Mark of the Wild**

#### Weaknesses:
- Relatively low initial threat output
- Lack of accessible crowd control options
- Limited flexibility in talent tree
- Absence of unique tank utilities

### When to Play This Spec
- Optimal against encounters with primarily physical or melee damage

### Playstyle Description
- Guardian Druids function as a sturdy tank, absorbing damage through high health and armor, and generating rage for both defensive and offensive capabilities. This specialization focuses on maintaining threat via damage-over-time abilities while managing immediate damage intake through cooldowns.

---

## ROTATION & ABILITIES

### Complete Priority Rotation
1. Maintain **Moonfire**
2. Maintain 5 stacks of **Thrash**
3. Cast **Heart of the Wild** off cooldown
4. Cast **Barkskin** off cooldown
5. Cast **Maul** when possible
6. Cast **Mangle**
7. Cast **Thrash**
8. Cast **Moonfire** (with **Galactic Guardian** proc)
9. Cast **Incarnation: Guardian of Ursoc**
10. Cast **Rage of the Sleeper**
11. **Swipe** - lowest priority

### Exact Cooldown Usage and Timing
- **Barkskin**: 20% damage reduction for 12 seconds; 60-second cooldown
- **Survival Instincts**: 50% damage reduction for 6 seconds; 3-minute cooldown, 2 charges
- Use defensives proactively based on anticipated damage or spikes.

### Single Target Rotation (Detailed Step-by-Step)
1. Cast **Growl**
2. Cast **Heart of the Wild**
3. Cast **Moonfire**
4. Cast **Thrash**
5. Cast **Incarnation: Guardian of Ursoc**
6. Cast **Thrash**
7. Cast **Mangle**
8. Cast **Thrash**
9. Cast **Rage of the Sleeper**
10. Cast **Maul (x3)**
11. Cast **Mangle**
12. Cast **Thrash**
13. Cast **Maul (x3)**

### AoE/Cleave Rotation (Detailed Step-by-Step)
1. Pull mobs with **Moonfire**
2. Cast **Barkskin**
3. Cast **Thrash**
4. Cast **Mangle** to generate rage
5. Dump rage into **Raze** while maintaining 1-3 stacks of **Ironfur**
6. Cast **Thrash**
7. Cast **Mangle**
8. Dump rage into **Raze** again

### Opening Rotation/Opener Sequence
#### Single-Target Opener
1. **Growl**
2. **Heart of the Wild**
3. **Moonfire**
4. **Thrash**
5. **Incarnation: Guardian of Ursoc**
6. **Thrash**
7. **Mangle**
8. **Thrash**
9. **Rage of the Sleeper**
10. **Maul (x3)**

### Key Abilities
- **Ironfur**: Increase Armor by 112% of Agility for 7 seconds
- **Frenzied Regeneration**: Heal for 20% of max health over 3 seconds, costs 10 Rage, max of 2 charges.
- **Barkskin**: 20% damage reduction for 12 seconds
- **Survival Instincts**: 50% damage reduction for 6 seconds

### Ability Interactions and Synergies
- Use **Moonfire** alongside **Galactic Guardian** for additional rage generation.
- Maintain **Thrash** to keep Rage flowing while ensuring adequate stacks for **Ironfur**.

### Rotation Conditionals (When to Use What)
- Use **Ironfur** based on incoming damage; if a spike is expected, pool Rage to stack multiple uses.
- **Frenzied Regeneration** should be used proactively or reactively based on anticipated or recent damage.

### Resource Management
- **Rage Generation**: 
  - Start with 0, max out at 100.
  - Gain Rage via auto-attacks, casting spells like **Mangle** (10 Rage), and others.
  - Avoid capping Rage while ensuring enough for defensive measures like **Ironfur** or **Frenzied Regeneration**.

---

## ADDITIONAL INFO

### Tips and Tricks
- Monitor Rage carefully; prioritize using it when you can avoid capping while also preparing for defensive needs.
- Always use **Barkskin** when anticipating high damage or Magic types.

### Common Mistakes
- Forgetting to maintain **Moonfire** or **Thrash** stacks.
- Overlapping cooldowns unnecessarily can lead to wasted defensive resources.
- Not pooling Rage for spike damage situations.

### Macros
- Set macros to track important procs like **Galactic Guardian** or **Tooth and Claw** to maximize damage efficiency.

### Addons
- Use WeakAuras for tracking procs to optimize rotation strategy.
- Consider a damage meter to ensure you are performing adequately in raids or Mythic+.

### Other Relevant Information
- After learning basics, advanced techniques like **Catweaving** or strategic use of cooldowns can be explored for higher DPS while tanking.
- Explore your talent choices and their impacts on your rotation and survivability.
